Introduction

Lore

Deep within the jungles of Venus lies a sanctuary, filled with mystery, wonder and great evil. A vault of unimaginable power, where the Vex, a race of trans-temporal cybernetic machines rule as masters of time and space. To understand the Vault of Glass, you must understand the Vex and the Pattern.

To the Vex, there exists only the Pattern. An absolute truth of perfection that has only one form; one future. Yet there are also anomalous factors in the equation. Glitches in the system that would unravel everything the Vex hold sacred. Because of this, the Vex seek to cut anything away that doesn't fall in line with the Pattern. Despite their incredible numbers and immeasurable levels of intelligence however, the Vex are still bound by the dimensional laws that bind our universe together. This is an arbitrary principle that the Vex aim to absolve.

In our reality, the Vex have their limits. But in the Vault of Glass, they rule reality in its absolute form. In the Vault, they are the masters of existence itself. Seeking to understand this place, many have led expeditions inside the Vault of Glass, such as Kabr the Legionless, Pahanin and Praedyth. But any who entered the heart of the Vault eventually became lost to time, even if their strength in the Light was unmatched.

Now, the Vanguard has sent you and five other Guardians into the Vault of Glass to stop whatever it is the Vex are planning. While the Vex may only be able to alter reality inside the Vault, who's to say they won't find a way to replicate their discoveries into our universe?



Synopsis

Released on September 16th 2014, the Vault of Glass is the first of the four Raids in Destiny to have been released. The Raid can only be unlocked after completing the Main Storyline of the Destiny 1.

Here are the level requirements for each version of the Raid:

Exotic of the Raid

The most powerful Exotic Weapon to use in the Vault of Glass is arguably the Gjallarhorn. In addition to the intrinsic perk, Wolfpack Rounds, the Gjallarhorn can deal a massive amount of damage to a target, has great crowd control possibilities and tracking rounds, leading to more accurate shots when zoomed in.

Many of the Vault of Glass' unique enemies and The Templar have large health-pools and small critical spots. The Gjallarhorn can deal large burst damage with each rocket and doesn't require players hitting these spots to achieve this.

With the release of Destiny's fourth expansion, Rise of Iron, the Gjallarhorn is now easily obtainable to all players and you are not required to complete a Raid to get it.



Unique Raid Relic:

The Aegis

Found within several encounters of the Vault of Glass is 'The Aegis'. It is a Shield-based Weapon that can counteract many mechanics found in the Encounters of the Vault of Glass. Here are the controls to using this weapon:

Melee Attack (On Land): The Player executes a Melee-Bash attack that can be used in combination three more times. (Bash-Bash-Bash).

Melee Attack (In Mid-Air): The Player executes an aerial dash-attack, which also allows them to hover for a short bit.

Primary Fire (On Land): The Player executes a powerful Shield-Bash that can instantly kill weaker enemies.

Primary Fire (In Mid-Air): The Player executes an Area-of-Effect Shockwave that can instantly kill weaker enemies.

Grenade: The Player creates an AOE barrier around them that protects them and anyone inside the barrier from any damage. Additionally, the barrier also creates a Cleanse effect, removing important de-buffs in some of the Encounters. The Cleanse effect cannot be used while sprinting and/or in mid-air.

Super: Shoots a powerful Orb of Light that targets ahead of the user in a straight line, tracking any enemies nearby.



Enemies:

Goblins are used as cannon-fodder in the Vault of Glass Raid, serving as an excellent way for players to generate Orbs of Light and Super Energy. Be wary, however, as Goblins can still prove to be formidable force, as their large numbers can easily overwhelm a player if left unchecked.

Hobgoblins are one of the more dangerous enemies you'll find the Vault of Glass. Their Solar Line Rifle attacks will very often three-shot you if you're out in the open. Unlike various Patrol and Story Mission locations, Vex Hobgoblins have very specific spawn placements in the Vault of Glass.

Minotaurs are the bulkiest and most dangerous of the regular enemies you will face in the Vault of Glass. Their large health and powerful Void Bolt attacks make them very dangerous foe in close-proximity.

Harpies, like Goblins, are used a cannon-fodder in some instances of the Raid, yet still can prove to be very dangerous to players in large groups. Additionally, Harpies can move extremely fast and have a rapid solar attack that can easily take down a player's shields, leaving other enemies to finish the job.

In addition to these default enemy types, the Vault of Glass also contains some unique enemy variations, each possessing their own mechanics and troubles to players.

Praetorian

Praetorians are a unique, Major-Class variation of the Minotaur enemy found in various parts of the Vault of Glass Raid. Save for their unique coloration and aggressive nature (they often come into close-proximity to players to deal heavy damage), they are no different than a regular Major-Class Minotaur. Their unique mechanic in the Raid, however, is the ability to deactivate Sync Plates when standing inside one.


Supplicant

Supplicants are a unique variation of the Harpy enemies. In addition to possessing a regular Harpy's attack patterns, Supplicants can detonate themselves in close-proximity. If a player is right next to them and hit by the explosion, they will instantly be killed (unless they have defensive buffs applied and/or are blocking with a Sword).


Fanatic

Fanatics are an extremely dangerous, Major-Class variation of the Goblin enemy. Fanatics behave like Vex Goblins without their heads and often charge towards the player. Much like a Supplicant, if a Fanatic comes into close-proximity of a player, they will explode, dealing a large amount of damage. Additionally, when a Fanatic dies, it will leave a small pool of corrupted, green energy known as a Pool of Negation. If a player touches this pool, they will be affected with the debuff, Marked for Negation.


Oracle (Yellow)

Yellow Oracles are stationery enemies found in the Vault of Glass that are only found in two encounters on Normal Mode and one on Hard Mode. Oracles have pre-determined spawns and cannot attack players. However, if left unchecked, Yellow Oracles will apply the debuff, Marked for Negation to the player.


Oracle (Red)

Red Oracles, much like Yellow Oracles, are stationery enemies found in the Vault of Glass that are found in one encounter on Normal Mode and two on Hard Mode. They behave exactly like Yellow Oracles, except when a Red Oracle activates, it will mark the player with the Prophecy of Doom debuff, draining your Super energy and instantly killing you shortly afterwards.


Gatekeeper

Gatekeepers are a unique, Major-Class enemy variation of the Vex Hydra. Aside from the name and special appearance, they behave no differently than a regular Major-Class Hydra.


Precursors & Descendants

Precursors and Descendants are unique, Major-Class enemy variations of the Vex Goblins, Hobgoblins and Minotaurs. Aside from the names and special appearances, they behave no differently than their regular counterparts.



Final Notes:

The Vault of Glass Raid is filled with powerful enemies that possess large health-pools and strong attacks. Additionally, the locations you will be fighting in often include multiple narrow passageways, where enemy ambushes are very likely to occur. Because of this, the most effective way to complete the Vault of Glass' encounters is to play defensively and conservatively. Ensure your weapons and armour allow you to deal high-burst damage in close-proximity, and always keep a look out on your surroundings. By keeping tabs on the enemy spawns during an encounter, you won't be overwhelmed as the fight continues.

Close-Range weapons such as Shotguns, Snipers and Swords work wonders on many of enemies in the Vault of Glass, and will save you a lot of hassle during the Raid's more challenging encounters.

Constructing the Spire

Lore

Within the Waking Ruins of the Ishtar Sink, the entrance to Vault of Glass lays dormant. It is among the most sacred of locations for the Hezen Corrective and the Vex are determined to keep any unwanted anomalies in their pattern out. However, there exists a means to forcefully open the Vault. By using the Vex's own technology against them, the Guardians can construct a key that'll open the way to the heart of the Vault.

Synopsis

Serving as the first encounter of the Vault of Glass Raid, players are tasked with the goal of opening the door to the Vault of Glass and facing the darkness of the Vex within. To do this, they will need activate and protect three Sync Plates found around the Waking Ruins. When all three are active, a giant Vex Spire in the centre will begin to construct. During this time, a series of Vex enemies will spawn near each Sync plate and attempt to deactivate the Plates and disrupt the construction process.

Once the Spire has been fully built, all enemies on the map will disappear and the encounter will be complete.

Something to note is that the Waking Ruins is NOT a Darkness Zone, so respawning restrictions are not in effct here. Additionally, because the Waking Ruins is also a public playspace, Fireteams may also run into other players partaking in other activities. Furthermore, these random players may even help your Fireteam construct the Spire.



Exotic of the Encounter

When it comes to dealing close-range, single-target damage, nothing compares to the Exotic Sword: The Raze-Lighter. Capable of killing a Major-Class in two fell strikes, the Raze-Lighter is perfect for tackling the Praetorians that attempt to deactivate the Sync plates.

Additionally, the Raze-Lighter is also very good at crowd-controlling other Vex enemies, such as Goblins and Hobgoblins. It can also be somewhat effective against Harpies, but the Raze-Lighter can have trouble hitting airborne enemies.

Guide:

When all six players have formed a Fireteam, selected the Vault of Glass Raid on the Director Menu and begun the mission, they will spawn near the cliff-face of the Waking Ruins, directly opposite to the entrance of the Vault of Glass.

As soon as players have spawned in, they must immediately make their way to the three Sync plates dotted around the Vault of Glass entrance. This is because the enemies guarding the entrance to the Vault of Glass will immediately start spawning in and begin defending their respective Sync Plates. Here are the three Sync Plates that players will need to defend.


The Sync Plates

The most effective way to defend these Plates is to split your Raid team into three pairs of two and assign them to a corresponding plate (e.g. 2 Left, 2 Middle and 2 Right). Have one person in each pair stand inside or near the Sync Plate, whilst having the other stay on high ground to spot, kill and control any enemies their partners on the Plate can't see and/or attack.

Once a player stands on a Sync Plate, it will begin activating, as seen below:



One thing to note is that the white, holographic barrier surrounding the plate is only visual confirmation that signifies it has begun activating. This holographic barrier will continue to grow in height, until it reaches a certain point. Once this happens, the Sync Plate will become fully active and will no longer require a player to be standing inside it.

For the Spire to begin its construction, all three Sync Plates must be fully activated and remain like this until the Spire has finished building itself. If any of the three Sync Plates become deactivated, the Spire will stop forming and slowly begin to deconstruct itself. If this happens, simply have one person stand back on the Plate once it's clear of enemies and wait for it to become fully activated again.

During the encounter, a variety of enemies will spawn around four key locations of the Waking Ruins map, via Doorways and Vex Cloud Spawns.

Vex Spawn Areas

While these enemies mainly consist of Goblins, Hobgoblins and Harpies, there are Praetorians that will occasionally spawn in to try and put to a stop to you. Should a Praetorian make it past your defences and enter a Sync, they will deactivate it and begin deconstructing the Spire.

To stop this, you simply need to kill the Praetorian on the Sync Plate, then reactivate the Plate after it has been defeated.

After constructing the Spire and defending the Sync Plates for a few minutes, a message will appear stating that 'The Spire has been activated!' and will finish its construction. After which, all enemies in the Waking Ruins will de-spawn and the entrance to the Vault of Glass will open, effectively completing the Encounter.


Rewards

Absolutely none! :D


Final Thoughts:

The enemies in the Waking Ruins are low-levelled, so defeating enemies like Vex Goblins and Harpies is no big-deal. Your challenge here will come from dealing with the Praetorians, which have more health than the other enemies and tend to charge towards a Sync Plate. But if you have a weapon capable of dealing strong burst damage, you should be fine.

While this Encounter is the first of the Raid, it is important to take notes on how the Sync Plates and Praetorians work, as you will be working with these mechanics later in the Raid.

Trial of Kabr

Lore

Noone truly knows what the Trial of Kabr is. Some say it is the final resting place of Kabr, the Legionless. Others say it is a test, preparing curious followers for the horrors that lie deeper in the Vault of Glass..


Synopsis

The Trial of Kabr is a series of cavernous tunnels that lead deeper into the Vault of Glass. This area serves as a reprieve for players after completing their first encounter, as well as a chance for them to familiarize themselves with their surroundings. The majority of the Raid will take place inside these dark chasms, so slowly introducing players to this new environment is imperative.

The Trial of Kabr is NOT a Darkness Zone, so respawn restrictions are not put in place.



Guide:

To successfully navigate the Trials' dark passageways, make sure to keep a good eye on your surroundings and check for any dark corners in the rocks around you. There is only one path to take here, but it bends and swerves around every corner. Just continue making your way, until you make it to this room pictured above.

This is where you will find the very first Raid Chest. Once opened, this Chest remain accessible to players for a maximum of ten minutes. After that, the chest will despawn and you will lose your chance at obtaining the rewards if you didn't open it in that time.

After unlocking the chest, there are two pathways to take. The first is the regular path, located in a corridor just next to where the first chest spawns. Following this pathway will eventually take you to the main entrance of the Templar's Well. The second path will lead you to a secret corridor hidden just underneath the room you are currently in and can be seen here.


After making your way through this hidden entrance, you will have a series of semi-difficult jumps you will have to make to progress forward. The path will lead you downwards, so keep heading that way until you see a notification telling you that you have just entered the Templar's Well. This will signify that you are on the right path, so continue making your through the foliage-covered Vex structures until you reach the second Raid chest.


The chest here will yield the same rewards as the one before, with the same chance of obtaining an Exotic.

After opening the chest, continue through the various jumping obstacles forward, until you reach an opening in one of the walls. Jump over the chasm and continue walking through this hidden entrance. It will eventually lead you to a side passage near the Templar's Well.

Defend the Confluxes

Settings

Defend the Confluxes

Lore

Deep within the Vault of Glass stands an existence out of time; an anomaly, which cannot be understood. The Templar, protector of the inner sanctums of the Vault, stands between you and the heart of the Vault of Glass. Yet, The Templar does not truly perceive you as a threat to the Vex Collective Mind. You are merely a glitch that can be fixed.

By constructing a series of Confluxes scattered around the Templar's Well, the Templar aims to send other Vex programming units to sacrifice themselves to its Confluxes in the order to cut you away from their reality.



Synopsis

As the second Encounter of the Vault of Glass Raid, Defend the Confluxes is the first real challenge that players will have to face. To complete it, the Fireteam will have to defend a series of Vex Confluxes that spawn around the Templar's Well and prevent any enemies from sacrificing themselves inside.

After a Conflux has successfully been defended, it will disappear and the Templar will summon a legion of Vex enemies to slaughter you. After surviving these hordes of enemies, more Confluxes will spawn and players will have repeat the process. After three Conflux rotations have passed and the final wave of enemies disappear, the Encounter will be complete.

Defend the Confluxes is the first Darkness Zone of the Raid, so Respawn Restrictions are put in place. They are as follows:

(Requires another Player to Revive)

Hard Mode: No Revive

Exotic of the Encounter

During this encounter, there will be lots of enemies spawning in large packs, as well as larger enemies such as the Minotaurs and Fanatics that need to be killed as quickly as possible. The Gjallarhorn can do both with ease. In addition to having the intrinsic perk, Wolfpack Rounds, the Gjallarhorn can also deal a massive amount of damage to a single target, has a good Blast Radius to clear areas and tracking shots when zoomed in, leading to more accurate shots.



Encounter Map



Guide:

Upon landing in the Templar's Well, the Encounter will begin and the Templar will immediately spawn, summoning the first Conflux. This will spawn at the back of the room in the middle.

Unlike the Vex Confluxes found in Venus Patrol Missions, you can only allow a maximum of three Vex enemies to sacrifice themselves to a single Conflux. Any more than this and the Conflux will activate, giving you and Fireteam the Expunged debuff, which will quickly drain your Super Energy and kill your entire team. The only way to avert this fate is to stop the Vex from reaching these Confluxes at all

After the first Conflux spawns, several Vex enemies will begin appearing around various parts of the room, making their way towards it. To prevent them from sacrificing themselves, the Fireteam will need to split up into three teams of two and occupy three spaces on the map, much like how the team was arranged for the 'Constructing the Spire' Encounter (This time, 2 on the Left, 2 on the Right and 2 in the Back Middle).


One mechanic that players will need to be aware of is that throughout the Encounter, the Templar will periodically disappear and reappear in the fight to attack any players in its line of sight. The Templar can deal a massive amount of damage to a player if they've left themselves unguarded, so it's important to stay out of its line of sight and communicate with the team when it's spawned in and out of the fight.

As time passes and you continue defending the Conflux, Vex Fanatics will begin to spawn in an attempt to sacrifice themselves to the Conflux. Fanatics act exactly like a Headless Goblins, but will charge towards the plate with no restraint. If you kill a Fanatic or it comes into close contact and explodes, it will leave a pool of corrupted green energy known as a Negation Zone on the area where it died. Contacting this substance will give you the Marked for Negation Debuff, which can stack up to four times. On its own, contracting the Debuff won't do anything, save for a disgusting green overlay covering your screen when you have it. However, this Debuff will need to be removed as soon as possible, because of the mechanic known as the Ritual of Negation.


When cast, the Templar will channel an ability that instantly kills anyone with the Marked for Negation Debuff upon it's complete. To counteract this, players will need to walk into a Pool of Light directly underneath the Templar that can remove all stacks of the Debuff on contact. However, there is a limit to how many stacks the Pool of Light can remove and its continued usage will eventually lead to the Pool disappearing entirely. Thankfully, the Pool of Light will replenish itself for every Ritual of Negation cast by the Templar.



After successfully defending the Conflux for a few minutes, it and all the enemies on the map will disappear. The Templar will then reappear at the centre and begin to summon its legions. This mechanic involves a massive number of Vex enemies spawning beneath and around the Templar, with the sole intention of killing your entire team. When this happens, all players of the fireteam should return to defend the back-middle platform. By doing this, the team can bottle-neck the enemies, taking care of them with ease. Just be careful not to stay near these chokepoints and there are several Fanatics among the hordes of enemies. Crowd-controlling Supers like Void Tethers and Hammer of Sols work excellently for this phase.

Additionally, as more enemies spawn in, Major-Class Harpies and Supplicants will begin spawning in and make their way towards the player. These enemies will need to be taken down quickly as Supplicants can instantly kill a nearby player with their explosions and Harpies can deal a large burst of damage to anyone in its line of sight. Finally, the Templar will reappear again near the end of the phase to deal more even more damage, so repeat what you did before and stay out of its line of sight.


After some time, the enemies will stop spawning and the Templar will begin casting the Ritual of Negation once again. If you are Marked at this point, make your way towards the Pool of Light to cleanse yourself. After the Ritual is complete, the Conflux Defence Phase of the fight will begin again, only this time, there will be TWO Confluxes to defend.

These Confluxes will be located on both the Left and Right sides of the map, but it is still important for the team to keep the position roles that they had before. This is because Minotaurs will begin spawning in the middle, so there will need to be people killing them before they harm anyone on the Left and Right sides. Furthermore, Harpies will begin spawning on the Left and Right sides, so it's extremely important that players keep a keen eye on their surroundings.


The same phases as before will continue, which will eventually lead to the third and final phase, where players will have to defend THREE Confluxes. The positioning of the players remains the same, only this time they will have to pay extra attention to their surroundings.

Finally, once the Confluxes have been successfully defended and the Templar's Legions have been defeated, no more Confluxes will spawn the Encounter will be complete.

Final Thoughts:

The key to successfully completing this Encounter is to always keep an eye on your surroundings. Many of the tougher enemies that spawn, like the Minotaurs, Fanatics and Supplicants can often sneak up behind you if you aren't looking. When this happens, it is very likely that you will die, or waste a Super that could've used more conservatively.

Settings

Destroy the Oracles

Lore

After witnessing the Hezen Protective failing to erase you from its reality, The Templar has concluded you and your Fireteam to be a significant threat to the Vex Collective Mind. As a result, the Templar now intends on expunging the Guardians itself. However, for as powerful the Templar may be, the Axis cannot manipulate reality on its own. To succeed in obliterating you, the Oracles must be summoned, for only they can prophesize your downfall.



Synopsis

As the third Encounter, Destroy the Oracles takes place shortly after completing the Defend the Confluxes encounter. In this encounter, a unique set of enemies called Oracles will spawn throughout the Templar's Well map in various locations. These Oracles must be destroyed before the Templar begins casting the Ritual of Negation.

There are four waves of Oracles that spawn and within each phase, all Oracles must be destroyed before the Ritual of Negation is cast. If even one Oracle is unsuccessfully destroyed, it will disappear and all players will be affected with the Marked for Negated Debuff. Additionally, if an Oracle disappears, the Wave will reset and players will have to try again until they succeed.

Once the Fireteam has destroyed the fourth and final wave of Oracles, the Templar will cast its final Ritual of Negation and the encounter will be complete.

Destroy the Oracles is still a Darkness Zone in the Raid, so Respawn Restrictions are put in place. They are as follows:

Additionally, the Dark-Drinker deals Void damage, making it an absolute beast when dealing with the Void-Shield Minotaurs that spawn during this Encounter.

Lore

With the Templar, the Gorgon's evaded and the Gatekeepers destroyed, the master of the Vault of Glass itself appears before you. Atheon, Time's Conflux. This Axis Mind is the heart of the Vault of Glass and is a conduit for all Vex Timelines. To understand Atheon is to understand the unfathomable nature of the Vault. Given the chance, Atheon can thrust you and your Fireteam through random moments of time and erase you from your existence. But to do so, Atheon must rely on the will and prophecy of the Oracles

But when all seems lost, the light of Kabr's will shines once again through the darkest recesses of Vex time. Wield the power of the Aegis, and destroy the heart of the Hezen Corrective.



Synopsis

As the ultimate enemy and Final Encounter of the Vault of Glass, Atheon is a formidable enemy unlike any you have seen before.

When the fight begins, you will notice that Atheon will hardly take any damage. Atheon, unlike the Templar, is not invulnerable and instead possesses a massive health-pool. In this state, Atheon is impervious to weapon fire.

After a few seconds of waiting, Atheon will open up the Vex timestream and teleport three random players in your Fireteam to either Venus or Mars. Inside, a Aegis Relic will spawn and one of the three players teleported inside will need to equip and use it throughout the Encounter. Inside Venus/Mars, six Red Oracles will spawn in sequential order and MUST be destroyed before the Prophecy of Doom debuff is applied. While this is happening, the players inside the Venus/Mars realm must deal with both the Precursor/Descendant Vex inside and the Marked by the Void debuff.

As for the players who were not teleported, their mission is to open up respective Vex Gate of the realm the rest of the Fireteam were teleported to, as well as deal with continuous waves of Supplicants.

Once all Six RED Oracles have been destroyed, a tooltip will appear at the bottom of the screen that staes 'Guardians make their own fate.'. When this happens, all players will receive a buff known as Time's Vengeance. This buff will massively increase your weapon, while instantly giving you Super Energy for the duration the buff is active, allowing to constantly use your Super Abilities.

After Time's Vengeance expires, Atheon will once again teleport three players to either Vex or Mars and the process from before will repeat. This will continue until Atheon is destroyed, and when that happens, the Encounter will be complete and you will have finished the Vault of Glass Raid.

Legacy

The Vault of Glass arguably contains some of the game's most powerful Primary Weapons, specifically the Fatebringer and the Vision of Confluence.
